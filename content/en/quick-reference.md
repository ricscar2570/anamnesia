# Quick Reference

Everything you need during play. Print and keep at the center of the table.

## Roll Formula

1. Choose an Approach (free, +1, or +2 Fragments) · 2. Invest Fragments from Pool (1–4 dice) · 3. Roll the dice

| Die | Type | Effect |
|:----|:-----|:-------|
| 5–6 | Clear | A vivid memory |
| 3–4 | Hazy | An ambiguous memory |
| 1–2 | Traumatic | Generates 1 permanent Echo |

## Outcome Table

| Clear | Outcome | Consequence |
|:------|:--------|:-----------|
| 3+ | Full Success | Vivid, useful memory. No consequences. |
| 2 | Partial Success | Incomplete memory or with emotional cost. |
| 1 | Painful Truth | Real but painful memory. Possible Stress. |
| 0 | Memory Collapse | The memory overwhelms you. +1 Stress. Complication. |

## Push the Memory *(v2.0)*

After Painful Truth or Collapse: improve the result by 1 level. Cost: +1 Stress. You cannot Push a Partial Success.

## Current Pool

Base Pool (4 or 5) − Stress − Echo Penalty = Available dice (minimum 1)

Echoes: 0–2 no penalty · 3–5 penalty −1 die · 6–8 penalty −3 dice · 9+ out of play

## Stress

| Level | Effect |
|:------|:-------|
| 0–2 | Control. Light tension. |
| 3 | Rising anxiety. Difficulty concentrating. |
| 4 | Partial Breakdown. Overwhelming emotions. |
| 5 | Final Breakdown. The character enters crisis. |

**Recovering Stress:** Roleplay Archetype Vulnerability (−1 Stress, max 1/cycle) · Human Connection at end of cycle · Invoke the Anchor (1/game, requires Stress 2+)

## Special Mechanics *(v2.0)*

**Last Memory (1/game):** With Pool at 1 die, roll 3d6 ignoring penalties. Then +1 Stress.

**The Truth Always Emerges (Cycle 5 only):** A Collapse still reveals the memory, but costs +2 Stress.

## Game Phases

| Phase | Cycles | Cards | Atmosphere |
|:------|:-------|:------|:----------|
| Fog | 1–2 | Act I only | Vague impressions, confusion |
| Connections | 3–4 | Act I + II | Concrete details, tension |
| Revelations | 5 | Act II + III | Raw truth, confrontation |

## The Right-Hand Rule

The player to your right interprets your roll's result, not you.

*If you stall: ask for a sense, roll on the Sensory Generator, or pass the word.*

## Sensory Trigger Generator (1d6)

| d6 | Sense | Examples |
|:---|:------|:--------|
| 1 | Smell | Disinfectant, smoke, blood, perfume, burnt food |
| 2 | Sound | Buzzing, intercom, crying, breaking glass, sudden silence |
| 3 | Sight | Flickering fluorescent, cracked mirror, moving shadow |
| 4 | Touch | Cold, wet, a mark on the skin, gloved hands |
| 5 | Taste | Metallic, bitter, medicinal, salt, rust |
