# Universal Fragments *(New in v2.0)*

If you don't have printed Fragment Cards — or if you're playing an improvised scenario — use these tables. Roll 2d6: the first die indicates the row, the second the column. If you get a fragment already used, roll again.

## Act I — Fog (Cycles 1–2)

Cost: 1 Fragment to interact.

| | 1–2 | 3–4 | 5–6 |
|:--|:----|:----|:----|
| **1–2** | A smell that doesn't belong here. Describe it. | A distant, constant sound, like a hum or a breath. | An object out of place — something that shouldn't be here. |
| **3–4** | A mark on your skin you don't remember. | The light changes: a pulsing fluorescent, a shifting shadow. | A sudden physical sensation: cold, heat, dull pain. |
| **5–6** | A taste in your mouth — metallic, bitter, sickly sweet. | Someone or something is missing. An absence that weighs. | A déjà vu: you've been here before. But when? |

## Act II — Connections (Cycles 3–4)

Cost: 1–2 Fragments to interact.

| | 1–2 | 3–4 | 5–6 |
|:--|:----|:----|:----|
| **1–2** | A document with your signature. You don't remember signing it. | A photograph with all of you — and one extra person. | Two of you remember the same moment differently. Who's lying? |
| **3–4** | A name no one wants to say. Who is it? | The same mark, on another character's skin. | A recorded voice. It's yours — but you're speaking about yourself in third person. |
| **5–6** | A personal belonging of someone who isn't here. | A date written somewhere. What happened that day? | Someone has known something all along and never said it. |

## Act III — Revelations (Cycle 5)

Cost: 2–3 Fragments to interact. Remember: in Cycle 5, *The Truth Always Emerges*.

| | 1–2 | 3–4 | 5–6 |
|:--|:----|:----|:----|
| **1–2** | The definitive proof of what happened. Everyone +1 Stress. | You find who was missing. Not where you expected. | The diary, the video, the recording: the raw, unfiltered truth. +2 Stress. |
| **3–4** | Someone confesses. *"What did you do the night before?"* | The door is open. It always was. Why didn't you leave? | Everything you remembered was false. The truth is worse. |
| **5–6** | You discover your specific role. The guilt is personal. | *"If you could forget again, would you?"* — Those who forget lose their Echoes. Those who remember keep everything. | The final memory: everyone rolls all Fragments. 3+: complete memory. 1–2: the player to the right fills the gaps. 0: it's worse. +2 Stress. |
