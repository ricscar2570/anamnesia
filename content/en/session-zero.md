# Session Zero

Before playing, dedicate 30–45 minutes to group calibration. This step is **mandatory and non-negotiable**.

AnamnesiA deals with themes of trauma, guilt, violence, and loss. Without a clear agreement on boundaries, the game can cause harm instead of telling a story.

If anyone doesn't feel comfortable during Session Zero, the game doesn't start. No exceptions.

## Structure (30–45 minutes)

| Time | Phase | Activity |
|:-----|:------|:---------|
| 0–10 min | Introduction | Explain what AnamnesiA is, its themes, why safety matters |
| 10–20 min | Lines & Veils | Discuss limits and implement safety tools |
| 20–30 min | Character Creation | Each player chooses Archetype, Anchor, fills in their sheet |
| 30–40 min | Calibration | Tone, intensity, confirm everyone is ready |

## Safety Tools

**X-Card.** Place a card with an X at the center of the table. Anyone can touch it — or say "X" — at any time to signal that content is making them uncomfortable. No explanation required. The scene changes immediately.

**Lines & Veils.** Lines = themes you don't want in the game, ever. Veils = themes that can exist in the story but happen "offscreen," without explicit detail. Go around the table. Write Lines and Veils on a sheet visible to everyone.

Here are the categories to discuss — you don't need to cover them all, but at least review them:

| Category | Line Examples | Veil Examples |
|:---------|:-------------|:-------------|
| Violence | Violence against children, torture | Physical violence mentioned but not described |
| Mental health | Graphic self-harm | Mention of disorders without clinical detail |
| Death | Animal death, detailed child death | Accidental death without description |
| Relationships | Explicit domestic abuse | Betrayal mentioned without scenes |

**Emotional Check-in.** Every 30–45 minutes, the Memory Keeper calls a short break (2–3 minutes). Questions: *"How is everyone doing? Does anyone need to slow down? Is there anything you'd like to adjust?"*

## Tone Calibration

Discuss the intensity level together. No formal questionnaire needed — just an honest conversation.

| Level | Description | For whom |
|:------|:-----------|:---------|
| Light | Psychological thriller focused on mystery. Uncomfortable but not devastating revelations. | New players or groups preferring less intensity |
| Medium | Balance between mystery and emotional exploration. Space for redemption and catharsis. | Standard AnamnesiA experience |
| Intense | Full psychological horror. No guarantee of a happy ending. | Experienced players, prepared for heavy content |

## Final Checklist

Before starting, verify:

☐ Everyone understands the game's themes · ☐ Lines & Veils defined and recorded · ☐ At least two safety tools implemented (X-Card + one other) · ☐ Each player has chosen an Archetype and an Anchor · ☐ Intensity level agreed upon · ☐ Everyone knows they can step away at any time

## Post-Game Debrief

After the game, dedicate 10–15 minutes to talking out of character. Simple but essential.

Questions: *How do you feel now? What moments struck you most? Was anything too intense? What will you take away from this story?*

If the game was particularly heavy, do something light together — a funny video, grab a snack, talk about something else. It helps to disconnect from the character and return to the real world.
