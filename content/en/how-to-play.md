# How to Play

A game of AnamnesiA lasts **5 cycles** of 15–20 minutes each, for a total of 90–150 minutes. Each cycle represents a wave of memories returning to the surface.

## The Three Phases

As cycles advance, memories become sharper and more painful:

**Phase 1: Fog (Cycles 1–2)** — Fragments are vague, sensory, ambiguous. Sensations rather than facts. Use only Act I Cards.

**Phase 2: Connections (Cycles 3–4)** — Details emerge: names, places, relationships. Memories begin to link together. Act I + II Cards.

**Phase 3: Revelations (Cycle 5)** — The raw truth. What really happened. Act II + III Cards. In this phase, the truth always emerges — even when you wish it wouldn't (see *The Truth Always Emerges*).

## Dealing Fragment Cards

At the start of each cycle, the Memory Keeper shuffles the deck matching the current phase and draws a number of cards equal to the **number of players plus one**. Cards are placed face-up in the center of the table. During the Exploration phase, any player may choose to interact with a visible card, following its prompts and spending the required Fragments. Once a card has been explored, it is discarded. Unused cards at the end of the cycle are shuffled back into the deck.

*If you don't have printed Fragment Cards, you can use the Universal Fragment Tables (ch. 14).*

## The Memory Keeper

Each cycle, a different player takes the role of Memory Keeper. This is not a Game Master: it's a **rotating facilitator**. The role passes clockwise after each Reset.

**The Memory Keeper's responsibilities:** Manage the timer and pace the cycle's phases. Read aloud the Echoes recorded in the previous cycle. Draw and lay out the Fragment Cards for the current cycle. Facilitate time distribution.

The Memory Keeper plays their own character normally during the Exploration phase. They can roll dice, interact with cards, and roleplay their Archetype like everyone else.
